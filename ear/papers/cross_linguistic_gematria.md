## Abstract

We present statistical analysis of numerical values assigned to words across Hebrew (Gematria), Greek (Isopsephy), and Arabic (Abjad) systems. Despite independent development across millennia and geographic isolation, semantically related words yield identical or harmonically related values at rates exceeding chance. We propose this reflects either (1) deep linguistic universals, (2) cross-cultural contact not captured in historical record, or (3) encoding of universal semantic structures in the development of alphabetic systems.
